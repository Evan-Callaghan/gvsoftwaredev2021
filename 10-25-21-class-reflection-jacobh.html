Kanban:

  • Kanban is a task organization and management system used by development teams to maximize efficiency. On the surface Kanban looks similar to Scrum in many ways however there are actually quite a few differences. Firstly, Kanban has a slightly less structured approach. In Kanban there are not predefined roles. Team members are encouraged to mix things up and assist other team members who are struggling on a particular task. Likewise, Kanban has a continuous task delivery system in place. Scrum on the other hand has set sprint lengths. Tasks in Kanban are taken on one at a time and the next one is picked up whenever the previous task has been comleted. This system allows Kanban to be more flexible with alterations to tasks and assignments being made on the spot. For this reason, Kanban is best for teams developing new products with many different and changing reuirements while Scrum might be a better fit for teams that have similar structure or requirements accross most of their projects.
